METROPOLITAN SHANGHAI

Metropolitan Shanghai

Why Shanghai?

Shanghai is widely regarded as the citadel of China's model economy with the implementation of the reform and open-up policy, also functioning as one of the most important cultural, commercial, financial, industrial and communications centers of China. There are few cities could evoke so much history, excess, glamour, mystique and exotic elements.

History

Development

1950

People's Republic of China

Shanghai

In the 1950s, The People's Republic of China is founded. The original municipalities were abolished, with only Shanghai and Beijing remained. Another significant change in Shanghai in this period was the integration of Jiading, Baoshan, Songjiang, Jinshan and other 9 counties, Chongming in Nantong, which is a part of Jiangsu Province, ten counties in total number. The area of Shanghai has soared from 636 sqk to 5910 sqk, that works to maintain the Shanghai status as the Industrial Center.

1978

Opening Up Stage

Challenge

In 1978, Deng has announced the Opening Up policy in China which promoted the rapid development of Guangdong Province. The status of Shanghai as the economic center in China has been challenged by a number of southern cities, especially the economic zones that set up with this policy.

1993

Reformation and Development

Pudong

After entering the 1990s, Shanghai has become the most important economic, financial and tading center again. In 1993, Pudong has been exploiated, which is accelerating the pace of urban development and became one of the most important districts in Shanghai. The annual GDP growth per capita is above 6%.The construction of connection channel between Pudong and Puxi also marked the prosperity. In November 1994, the Oriental Pearl TV Tower, which is one of the most famous landmark was completed. The Jinmao Tower was officially opened for business on March 18, 1999 and the Shanghai Conference Center opened in August 1999.

2010

Rapid Development

Expo Exhibition

The 2000s is of great meaning for Shanghai. On October 21, 2001, Shanghai hosted the 9th APEC Informal Meeting and it has increased its internation influence a lot. Since then, Shanghai, even China has experienced rapid development of economy and whole society. In 2008, the flattened Shanghai World Finical Center became the tallest building in China at that time (later surpassed). In 2010, Shanghai is mainly for the World Expo Exhibition. In the same year, there are many landmark buildings built such as Wheelock International Plaza and International Financial Center (IFC).

2020

Nowadays

Skyscapers

Over the past decades, Shanghai has experienced rapid development. The new 125-story Shanghai Tower, China's tallest building and the world's second tallest skyscrapers, at 632 meters (2073 ft), is finished in 2016, loacted in Pudong district. The SK, White Magnolia Plaza and Hongkong XingYe Center was also built in this stage. The skyscapers has formed the symbol of modern Shanghai and is defining the city's landscape to a new level.

Project Info

Shanghai stands out in many ways, but one thing that redefining as an iconic city is about its unique and various urban elements and activities. With the rapid development over the decade, it has experienced a great change. This project aims to find out the urban development of Shanghai from various perspective, mainly from 1950 to nowadays.
